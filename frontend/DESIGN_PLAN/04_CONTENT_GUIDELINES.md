# 04_CONTENT_GUIDELINES.md — Copy, Messaging & Content Strategy

## Brand Voice

**Tone**: Premium, artisanal, meticulous, exclusive  
**Voice**: Sophisticated but warm, not corporate or stuffy  
**Style**: Fine dining language (tasting notes, sourcing, craft), storytelling  
**Audience**: High-income professionals (30-50) who value craft and exclusivity

---

## DO's & DON'Ts

### DO:
- ✅ Use sensory language ("hints of vanilla", "crispy edge with soft center")
- ✅ Reference sourcing and process ("ethically sourced Madagascar vanilla")
- ✅ Emphasize exclusivity ("member-only flavors", "limited drops")
- ✅ Build narrative (founder's story, craft philosophy)
- ✅ Be specific (not "premium ingredients" but "single-origin cocoa from Ecuador")

### DON'T:
- ❌ Use marketing clichés ("world-class", "game-changing", "revolutionary")
- ❌ Over-explain or be condescending
- ❌ Use excessive exclamation points or emojis
- ❌ Mention discounts or "sales" (reframe as "membership benefits")
- ❌ Use generic product descriptions
- ❌ Sound corporate or formal

## Product Copy Template: "Tasting Notes"

### Format
Use fine dining language for product descriptions.

### Template
```
**[Product Name]**

Tasting Notes:
• Aroma: [Scent description]
• Flavor: [Primary + secondary flavors]
• Texture: [Mouthfeel]
• Finish: [Aftertaste]

The Craft:
[1-2 sentences on sourcing, technique, or time involved]

Why It Matters:
[1 sentence on significance or inspiration]
```

### Example: Vanilla Cookie
```
**Madagascar Vanilla Crescent**

Tasting Notes:
• Aroma: Rich, sweet vanilla with subtle caramel undertones
• Flavor: Creamy vanilla with hints of salted butter and delicate floral note
• Texture: Crispy golden exterior, tender crumb inside with light almond finish
• Finish: Smooth, lasting vanilla warmth with a whisper of sea salt

The Craft:
Hand-rolled with Vermont butter and real Madagascar vanilla pods sourced directly from artisanal growers. Each batch rests for 24 hours to develop the complex flavor profile.

Why It Matters:
Our signature cookie celebrates the founder's first trip to Madagascar and ethical farming partnerships.
```

## Tone Examples

### Announcing a New Product
```
"Introducing [Product]: [One-line sensory description]. Limited drops begin [Date] for members, public release [Date + 3]. This flavor celebrates [inspiration/story]."
```

### Building FOMO / Scarcity
```
"Members don't watch products sell out. They buy them three days early."
```

### Emphasizing Craft
```
"Quality takes time. Each [product] takes [X hours/days] of craftsmanship, [specific technique], and [specific ingredient]. We refuse to rush."
```
